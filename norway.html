Norway

About Norway

Capital: Oslo


Official language: Norwegian


Norway is a Scandinavian country encompassing mountains, glaciers and deep coastal fjords. Oslo, the capital, is a city of green spaces and museums. Preserved 9th-century Viking ships are displayed at Oslo's Viking Ship Museum. Bergen, with colorful wooden houses, is the starting point for cruises to the dramatic Sognefjord. Norway is also known for fishing, hiking and skiing, notably at Lillehammer's Olympic resort.

My trip to Norway

Travel length: 3 weeks, August 2012


City I travelled to: Oslo, Bergen

Attractions

Preikestolen

A stunning cliff rising 604 meters above Lysefjorden, Preikestolen offers breathtaking views of the Norwegian landscape.

Geirangerfjord

A picturesque 15-kilometer-long fjord in the Sunnmøre region, known for its dramatic beauty and crystal-clear waters.

Fish Market in Bergen

Dating back to the 1200s, the Fish Market in Bergen offers fresh seafood and local farm produce in a charming setting.

Bryggen Hanseatic Wharf

A UNESCO-listed heritage site in Bergen, Bryggen is a series of Hanseatic commercial buildings with rich historical significance.

Cuisines

Fårikål

Norway's national dish, Fårikål, is a hearty combination of lamb, cabbage, and potatoes, traditionally enjoyed in the colder months.

Lefse

A traditional Norwegian flatbread made with flour, potatoes, butter, and cream, offering a neutral base for sweet or savory toppings.

Gravlax

Gravlax or graved salmon is a Nordic dish consisting of salmon that is cured using a mix of salt, sugar and dill. It is garnished with fresh dill or sprucetwigs and may occasionally be cold-smoked afterwards.

Pinnekjøtt

A Christmas Eve favorite, Pinnekjøtt is a dish of salt-cured rack of lamb steamed over birch twigs, offering a rich and salty flavor.
