Netherlands

About Netherlands

Capital: Amsterdam


Official language: Dutch


The Netherlands, a country in northwestern Europe, is known for a flat landscape of canals, tulip fields, windmills and cycling routes. Amsterdam, the capital, is home to the Rijksmuseum, Van Gogh Museum and the house where Jewish diarist Anne Frank hid during WWII. Canalside mansions and a trove of works from artists including Rembrandt and Vermeer remain from the city's 17th-century "Golden Age."

My trip to Netherlands

Travel length: 2 weeks, 2013


City I travelled to: Amsterdam

Attractions

Amstel River

Take a scenic stroll along the iconic Amstel River, known for its picturesque views and charming bridges.

Rijksmuseum

Explore the rich cultural history of the Netherlands at the Rijksmuseum, home to a vast collection of Dutch masterpieces.

Van Gogh Museum

Immerse yourself in the artistry of Vincent van Gogh at the Van Gogh Museum, showcasing the life and works of the renowned Dutch painter.

Royal Palace Amsterdam

Step into regal elegance at the Royal Palace Amsterdam, a stunning architectural masterpiece in the heart of the city.

Cuisines

Stroopwafels

Indulge in the sweetness of Stroopwafels, a Dutch delight made of two thin waffle layers filled with caramel syrup. Enjoy them with a hot cup of coffee for a perfect treat.

Pannenkoeken

Savor the Dutch version of pancakes with Pannenkoeken. Larger and thinner than American pancakes but thicker than French crÃªpes, these pancakes are enjoyed throughout the day, especially for dinner.

Poffertjes

Delight in the cuteness of Poffertjes, small and fluffy Dutch pancakes made with yeast and buckwheat flour. Served with a lump of butter and powdered sugar, these treats are often enjoyed at outdoor stands during winter.

Bitterballen

Experience the savory goodness of Bitterballen, deep-fried meat-based balls served with mustard. The crunchy breadcrumb coating gives way to a hot and flavorful filling made of beef, beef broth, butter, and flour.
